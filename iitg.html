About IIT Guwahati

Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, established in 1994, is one of India’s premier institutions in the field of higher education. Situated along the banks of the mighty Brahmaputra River, the institute offers a conducive environment for academic pursuit and intellectual growth. Renowned for its academic rigor, cutting-edge research infrastructure, and interdisciplinary orientation. The institute fosters innovation that addresses pressing societal and technological challenges. The institute is home to several centres of excellence and maintains robust collaborations with industry and academic institutions around the world.

How to reach IIT Guwahati

From Airport: IIT Guwahati is approximately a 30-minute drive from the airport. App-based taxi services like Ola, Uber, and ASTC City Cabs are available at the airport and typically charge around ₹550–700 for a reserved ride to the campus. Prepaid taxis are also available just outside the arrivals gate.

Railway Station: Taxis and auto-rickshaws are readily available for commuting from the railway station to the IIT Guwahati campus. Additionally, the institute operates its own transportation between the campus and the city. The buses depart from a stop opposite Hotel President in Panbazar, which is about a 15-minute walk from the railway station. An updated bus schedule will be shared a week before the conference.

Weather in Guwahati

Guwahati experiences a humid subtropical climate. In June, the city marks the onset of the monsoon season, characterized by high humidity and substantial rainfall. Average temperatures during this month range from lows of approximately 24.9°C to highs around 30.4°C. Rainfall is significant, with averages between 360 mm and 450 mm, occurring over approximately 15 to 20 days. Despite the frequent rain, there are about 13 hours of daylight daily, allowing ample time to explore the city's attractions.
